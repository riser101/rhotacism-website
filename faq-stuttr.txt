1. Is this program actually for adults/teens, or is it just for children?
Answer: You are looking at a program built by adults, for adults. Our founders, 
Deborah, Peter, and Jaime, are practicing clinicians who specialized in adult fluency disorders. 
They understand that adult stuttering involves complex emotional and situational factors that children's therapy doesn't always address. 
There are no cartoon characters here—just science-backed techniques, real-world conversation practice, and professional progress tracking 
designed for your lifestyle.


2. I’ve tried "fixing" my stutter before. Why should I believe your 94% success rate?
Answer: That skepticism is valid. The 94% "see results in 2 weeks" statistic isn't a marketing gimmick; it’s a reflection of our structured, 
sequential methodology. Unlike traditional therapy that might jump around, our 12-week protocol builds a foundation (breathing/pacing) 
before moving to advanced conversation. We see results quickly because you aren't guessing what to practice next—the clinical pathway 
tells you exactly what to do, and biweekly tracking holds you accountable, proving progress to yourself.


3. What if I can’t afford the $1,500–$3,000 for a private SLP? Is the $249 price tag too good to be true?
Answer: We are on a mission to democratize fluency. The high cost of traditional therapy is due to overhead (rent, commuting, admin). 
We removed that. You are paying for the clinical protocol developed by board-certified SLPs, delivered in a self-paced format. At $249, 
it’s the cost of roughly one traditional therapy session, but you get the entire 16-week system. Plus, you can test it risk-free with a 
7-day free trial to ensure it’s the right fit before you commit financially.


4. Can I really do this alone? I’m afraid I’ll practice the wrong thing and make my stutter worse.
Answer: You are not alone—you are guided. This is the difference between "self-help" and a "structured academy." You cannot practice 
the wrong thing because the app tells you exactly what to do each day. The program was designed by SLPs to ensure you are always building 
on a stable foundation. If you get stuck, the pathway adapts. It’s like having a clinical supervisor in your pocket, ensuring you don't 
reinforce bad habits.


5. What is actually involved in the "Free Assessment"?
Answer: The Free Assessment is your entry point to personalization. You will be prompted to record yourself speaking a few specific 
test words and sentences (in the privacy of your own space). Our algorithm analyzes your speech patterns—looking for specific disfluency 
types, tension points, and pacing issues. Based on this analysis, it generates your customized 12-week program, so you aren't working on 
"stuttering" in general, but on your specific speech pattern.


6. I’m terrified of being seen or judged. How private is this?
Answer: 100% private. We understand that the fear of judgment is a core component of stuttering. There are no face-to-face appointments, 
no waiting rooms, and no uncomfortable eye contact while you struggle with a word. All your recordings—from your initial assessment to your 
biweekly check-ins—are stored securely in your private portal. You share your progress with the algorithm and your own eyes only
 (unless you choose to share it with an SLP).


7. What if I have a bad day? Does the program account for relapses or setbacks?
Answer: Absolutely. Fluency is not linear. The program is built to handle the "bad days." The biweekly progress tracking is designed to 
catch fluctuations. If your speech feels harder one week, the data will show it, and the subsequent modules focus on resilience and recovery 
techniques. We teach you how to get back into your "flow" when life throws you off, rather than expecting perfection every single day.


8. I’m really busy. Is 15 minutes a day actually enough to change a lifelong habit?
Answer: Yes, because of neuroplasticity. Consistent, short bursts of practice are more effective for rewiring motor patterns than long, 
exhausting sessions once a week. The 15 minutes of "Daily Practice" are hyper-focused—tongue breathing, pacing strategies, or fluency shaping. 
It’s like going to the speech gym for a quick, high-intensity workout. You are more likely to stick with 15 minutes, and consistency is what 
builds the new, fluent muscle memory.


9. Do I need any special equipment or apps to use this?
Answer: All you need is your smartphone, tablet, or computer with a microphone (which almost all modern devices have). The platform works 
right in your browser or via our secure web app. You record audio directly on the device you already use every day. No need to buy headsets 
or download complicated software.


10. What happens after the 12 or 16 weeks? Will I lose my progress if I stop practicing?
Answer: The goal is to make fluency automatic so it becomes your default setting. In the final weeks of the program ("Real-World Application"), 
you transition from structured exercises to spontaneous conversation. Once the program ends, you own the skills. However, you retain access to 
the materials and your progress tracker for reference. If you ever feel a "rustiness," you can simply revisit a specific week's module for a 
refresher—it’s your toolkit for life.

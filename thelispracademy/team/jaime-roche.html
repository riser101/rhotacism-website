Advisory Board Member

Meet our clinical experts developing evidence-based speech therapy protocols

Jaime Roche

M.S., CCC-SLP, Loyola University Maryland
Speech Sound Disorders & Communication Development
ASHA Certified

About

Jaime Roche is a highly accomplished speech-language pathologist with over 20 years of clinical experience specializing in the comprehensive evaluation and treatment of speech sound disorders. Her dedication to evidence-based practice has made her a recognized leader in the field of communication development.

As an ASHA ACE Award recipient, Jaime has demonstrated exceptional commitment to advancing clinical excellence in speech-language pathology. Her expertise spans working with both children and adults, bringing a wealth of knowledge to every case she encounters.

Clinical Expertise

  • Comprehensive evaluation and treatment of speech sound disorders
  • Evidence-based therapy approaches for rhotacism and R sound production
  • Adult speech therapy and articulation remediation
  • Pediatric communication development
  • Motor speech disorders and phonological processes

Education & Credentials

Jaime earned her Master of Science degree in Speech-Language Pathology from Loyola University Maryland, one of the nation's premier programs for communication sciences. She holds the Certificate of Clinical Competence in Speech-Language Pathology (CCC-SLP) from the American Speech-Language-Hearing Association (ASHA).

Contributions to The Lispr Academy

At The Lispr Academy, Jaime plays a crucial role in developing and validating the clinical protocols that form the foundation of our 12-week treatment program. Her extensive experience with speech sound disorders ensures that every exercise and technique is grounded in proven therapeutic methods.

Her expertise helps translate decades of in-clinic experience into accessible, self-paced exercises that maintain the same rigor and effectiveness as traditional speech therapy sessions.
